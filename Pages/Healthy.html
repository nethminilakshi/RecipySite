Healthy Foods

Exploring healthy recipes can transform your meals into nutritious delights that nourish your body and tantalize your taste buds. Start your day with a delicious smoothie bowl, blending spinach, banana, and almond milk, then top it with fresh fruits, nuts, and seeds for added texture and flavor. For lunch, try a vibrant quinoa salad featuring colorful bell peppers, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, and a zesty lemon vinaigrette, packed with protein and fiber to keep you satisfied. For dinner, consider grilling salmon marinated in herbs and lemon juice, served alongside roasted vegetables like Brussels sprouts and sweet potatoes, providing a balanced meal rich in omega-3 fatty acids and essential vitamins.

Pumpkin Soup

Pumpkin soup is a creamy, comforting dish that embodies the essence of autumn with its warm, rich flavors and vibrant orange hue. Made primarily from pureed pumpkin, this soup often combines other wholesome ingredients such as onions, garlic, and spices like ginger, nutmeg, or cinnamon, which enhance its natural sweetness. The base can vary, with options like vegetable broth, chicken stock, or coconut milk providing depth and creaminess. Pumpkin soup is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients, including vitamins A and C, making it a healthy choice.

Served hot, it can be garnished with roasted pumpkin seeds, a swirl of cream, or a sprinkle of paprika to add visual appeal and a touch of flavor. This delightful soup is perfect as a starter for a festive meal or as a hearty lunch, inviting warmth and comfort on chilly days. Its versatility allows it to be enjoyed with crusty bread, served alongside a fresh salad, or even blended with other seasonal vegetables, making it a favorite for both casual dinners and special occasions.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2kg pumpkin, peeled, chopped
  • 1kg pumpkin or squash (try kabocha), peeled, deseeded and chopped into chunks
  • 700ml vegetable stock or chicken stock
  • 150ml double cream

For the croutons

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 slices wholemeal seeded bread, crusts removed
  • handful pumpkin seeds

How to Make Pumpkin Soup:

  1. Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a large saucepan, then gently cook 2 finely chopped
    onions for 5 mins, until soft but not coloured.
  2. Add 1kg pumpkin or squash, cut into chunks, to the pan, then carry on cooking for
    8-10 mins, stirring occasionally until it starts to soften and turn golden.
  3. Pour 700ml vegetable or chicken stock into the pan and season with salt and pepper.
    Bring to the boil, then simmer for 10 mins until the squash is very soft.
  4. Pour 150ml double cream into the pan, bring back to the boil, then pur√©e with a hand blender.
    For an extra-velvety consistency you can pour the soup through a fine sieve.
  5. Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a frying pan, then fry the bread until it starts to become crisp.
  6. Add a handful of pumpkin seeds to the pan, then cook for a few mins more until they
    are toasted. These can be made a day ahead and stored in an airtight container.
  7. Reheat the soup if needed, taste for seasoning, then serve scattered with croutons and
    seeds and drizzled with more olive oil, if you want.
